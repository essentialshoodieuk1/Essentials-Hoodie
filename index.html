What is an Essentials Hoodie & Who Makes Them?

An Essentials Hoodie is a minimalist streetwear hoodie created under the Fear of God Essentials line, designed by Jerry Lorenzo.

Fear of God Essentials sits under the main Fear of God label, which Jerry Lorenzo built around elevated basics—clean silhouettes, muted colors, oversized fits, and that luxury-street hybrid vibe. Essentials is basically the more accessible version. Same design language, lower price point, wider drops.

The hoodie itself is known for its heavyweight fleece, dropped shoulders, and bold but simple “ESSENTIALS” branding. Most pieces feature rubberized logos on the chest or back and a tonal aesthetic that avoids loud graphics.

It’s not hype for the sake of hype. The appeal is how easy it is to wear. You can throw it on with cargos, denim, shorts—doesn’t matter. It just works. If you are looking for the latest styles, check out our collection of Essentials Hoodie options. And yeah, drops sell out fast. Real fast.

Essentials Hoodie Sizing Guide: Do They Run Big?

Essentials hoodies generally run oversized by design, so most people should expect a relaxed, boxy fit rather than a regular slim fit.

Fear of God built this silhouette intentionally. It’s supposed to drape, stack at the sleeves, and sit loose on the body. If you’re used to standard Nike or Adidas sizing, this will feel noticeably roomier.

Are Essential Hoodies True to Size or Oversized?

They are oversized by default, and that’s the intended look—so going true to size gives you a baggy streetwear fit, while sizing down gives a cleaner, more structured silhouette.

Here’s how it usually plays out in real life:

  • True to size: You get the intended Essentials look—droopy shoulders, longer sleeves, and that boxy torso. This is what most streetwear fans go for.
  • Size down once: Cleaner fit, still relaxed but less dramatic. Good if you don’t want it swallowing your frame.
  • Size up: Very oversized. Almost runway loose. Works for layered fits but not practical for everyone.

If you’re unsure, think about how you like your hoodies to sit. Essentials isn’t trying to be fitted. It’s built for volume. For those looking to complete the full streetwear look, pairing it with an Essentials Tracksuit offers the perfect matching aesthetic.

How to Wash and Care For Your Hoodie

Essentials hoodies should be washed gently in cold water and air-dried to maintain fabric quality and protect the branding. The material is thick and premium, but the rubberized or flocked logos can wear out if you treat it like gym clothes.

Can Essentials Hoodies Go in the Washing Machine?
Yes, Essentials hoodies can go in the washing machine, but only on a cold, gentle cycle with the hoodie turned inside out. Follow this:

  • Turn hoodie inside out before washing
  • Use cold water only
  • Choose a gentle or delicate cycle
  • Use mild detergent (no bleach, no harsh chemicals)
  • Wash with similar colors to avoid dye transfer
  • Avoid overloading the machine

Drying matters even more than washing: Air dry flat or hang dry. Avoid tumble drying (heat can shrink fleece and damage logos). Don’t wring it out aggressively.

Legit Check: How to Spot a Fake Essentials Hoodie

The easiest way to spot a fake Essentials hoodie is by checking the logo quality, neck tag spacing, stitching consistency, and wash tag typography.

  1. Neck Tag & Branding Placement: Authentic hoodies have clean, evenly spaced “ESSENTIALS” lettering on the neck label.
  2. Rubberized Logo Quality: On legit pieces, the rubber logo feels solid, slightly raised, and cleanly molded.
  3. Stitching Around Shoulders and Pockets: Real Fear of God Essentials stitching is consistent and tight.
  4. Wash Tags and Care Labels: Authentic tags use precise, sharp font printing with consistent spacing.
  5. Fabric Weight and Feel: Real hoodies feel heavy and structured.
